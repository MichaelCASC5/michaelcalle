As you might already be able to tell, my name is Michael Calle. I was born in Queens, New York, in December of 2002. At a young age I discovered my urge to want to build things, and create things, but I always found myself limited in terms of resources and ability. When I was in middle school, I took a free introduction to computer science and programming course with the Bridge to Enter Advanced Mathematics (BEAM), and I immediately fell in love. I found myself coding and building programs in all the freetime I had available. I had this incessant urge to learn more and more, and this endless fire to continue to work to build greater and greater things.

I probably shouldn't speak in past tense, since that urge within me has not died to this day.

My Educational Career

At the moment, I'm a computer science major at Hunter College. It was about halfway through high school did I realize that computer science wasn't something I could just do for myself, to build things just for myself, but I could build things for people. I could help build the future. After moving on from AP Computer Science, and after investing many summers and winters in coding-related intensives and extracirriculars, I chose my major for the reason that I'd be able to continue doing computer science far into the future.

Although for many years I've pursued knowledge and education in programming, and now computer theory, I still don't have proper experience in the field. I'm hunting for opportunities and internships every day!
